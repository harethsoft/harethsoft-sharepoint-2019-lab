**Creating and Naming SharePoint Online Sites (Classic & Modern)**

 creation of SharePoint Online sites and naming conventions, here is the exhaustive step-by-step guide, 

**Step 1: Adopt a Small and Meaningful Naming Convention**
Always provide a short name (e.g., **HR** instead of Human Resource) because the first name you provide is used to generate the **permanent URL**, which cannot be changed later.

**Step 2: Eliminate Spaces and Special Characters**
Avoid using spaces or special characters in the site name; in **classic sites**, spaces are converted to **"%20"** in the URL, which looks unprofessional and cluttered.

**Step 3: Account for the 400-Character URL Limit**
Ensure the total length of your URL—including the site name, library, nested folders, and filenames—does not exceed the limit, which is now **up to 400 characters**.

**Step 4: Avoid Placeholder or "Test" Names**
Do not use names like "Marketing Test" if you plan to use the site for production later, as you **cannot remove "test" from the URL** once the site is created.

**Step 5: Deploy Classic Sites via the Admin Center**
Create **classic SharePoint sites** by navigating to "More features" in the SharePoint Admin Center and opening the "Classic site collection page" to select specific templates.

**Step 6: Select Specialized Classic Templates**
Choose from templates like the **Record Center** for legal or finance documents with built-in auditing, or the **Document Center** for managing large-scale content archives.

**Step 7: Choose Between Team and Communication Sites**
Select a **Modern Team Site** for internal collaboration where everyone contributes, or a **Communication Site** to broadcast information to a large audience where only a few members contribute.

**Step 8: Provision Modern Sites Directly from Active Sites**
Create a **Modern Team Site** via the "Active Sites" page in the Admin Center; unlike classic sites, the modern experience automatically **removes spaces** from the URL for a cleaner look.

**Step 9: Utilize Office 365 Group Creation**
Provision a site by creating an **Office 365 Group**; this automatically generates a modern SharePoint site along with a shared group email address and calendar.

**Step 10: Generate Sites via Microsoft Teams**
Build a team from scratch within **Microsoft Teams**, which instantly creates a corresponding **modern SharePoint site** in the backend to store all team-related files.
