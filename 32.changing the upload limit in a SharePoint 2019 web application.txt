 **changing the upload limit in a SharePoint 2019 web application**, here is the step-by-step guide for administrative configuration, 


**Step 1: Understand the Default Upload Limit**
Recognize that by default, the SharePoint 2019 on-premise environment allows you to upload data with a maximum size limit of **10 GB**. This applies whether data is uploaded directly or through an upload-download utility.

**Step 2: Access Central Administration**
Log in to your server and open the **SharePoint Central Administration** portal to begin the configuration process.

**Step 3: Navigate to Web Application Management**
Go to the **Application Management** section and click on the **Manage web applications** link.

**Step 4: Select the Target Web Application**
Identify and select the specific web application (e.g., your logistics and supply chain site) where you want to modify the data upload quantity.

**Step 5: Open General Settings**
Once the web application is selected, click on the **General Settings** option located in the ribbon at the top of the page.

**Step 6: Locate the Maximum Upload Size Field**
Scroll to the bottom of the General Settings window. You will find a field labeled **"Maximum upload size"** where the current limit is defined.

**Step 7: Define the New Upload Limit**
Change the value in the field to meet your specific requirements. For example, to reduce the limit from 10 GB to **2 GB**, you would enter **2048 MB** into the box.

**Step 8: Save the Configuration Changes**
Click the **OK** button at the bottom of the settings page to apply the new limit to your web application.

**Step 9: Verify the Change**
Confirm that the upload size limit has been successfully altered in the system. In this example, the limit is now strictly enforced at 2 GB instead of the original 10 GB.

**Step 10: Control User Data Uploads**
By adjusting these settings, you effectively control the amount of data users can upload at one time to your document libraries, helping to manage server resources and storage more efficiently.
