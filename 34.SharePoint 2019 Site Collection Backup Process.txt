**SharePoint 2019 Site Collection Backup Process**

Based on the source transcript about performing site collection backups, here is the step-by-step guide for securing your site data,

**Step 1: Access the Backup Interface**
Navigate to the central administration area designated for performing **site collection backups**.

**Step 2: Identify the Hosting Web Application**
Recognize the main **web application** that hosts your various site collections. This is the top-level container where your different sites are organized.

**Step 3: Review Available Site Collections**
Examine the list of available site collections within your environment. In a typical demonstration, you might see several sites (such as the six shown in the source) hosted under a single application.

**Step 4: Select the Specific Target Site**
Identify and select the specific site you wish to back up. For instance, you might choose the **root site** to ensure your primary landing page is secured.

**Step 5: Confirm Your Selection**
After highlighting the correct site collection, click **OK** to proceed to the configuration of the backup file.

**Step 6: Name the Backup File**
Provide a clear and descriptive name for the output file, ensuring you use the correct extension (e.g., **"root site.bak"**) so the system recognizes it as a backup file.

**Step 7: Initiate the Backup Job**
Click the button to **start the backup**. The system will place the job into a queue and begin processing the data.

**Step 8: Monitor the Completion Time**
Understand that the duration of the backup **depends entirely on the data size** of your site collection. While a small demonstration site may finish in a few seconds, larger production environments will take longer.

**Step 9: Verify the Backup File**
Navigate to your storage folder and **refresh the view** to confirm that the `.bak` file has been created successfully and is saved in the intended location.

**Step 10: Prepare for Future Restoration**
Note that while the backup is done through the interface, the **restoration process** typically requires using the **SharePoint Management Shell** and executing specific scripts to bring the site back online.
